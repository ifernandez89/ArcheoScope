# ArcheoScope Ethical Use Guidelines

**Version 1.0 | January 2026**

## Preamble

Archaeological heritage belongs to all humanity and requires protection, respect, and responsible stewardship. ArcheoScope is designed to support legitimate archaeological research and heritage management while preventing misuse that could harm archaeological sites or violate cultural rights.

## Core Ethical Principles

### 1. Archaeological Heritage Protection
- Archaeological sites are **non-renewable cultural resources**
- **Preservation takes precedence** over research convenience
- **Community rights** supersede research interests
- **Professional standards** must be maintained at all times

### 2. Scientific Integrity
- **Transparency** in methodology and limitations
- **Reproducibility** of analysis and results
- **Peer review** and professional validation
- **Uncertainty acknowledgment** in all interpretations

### 3. Cultural Respect
- **Indigenous rights** and traditional knowledge systems
- **Local community** heritage values and concerns
- **Cultural sensitivity** in research design and implementation
- **Collaborative approaches** with affected communities

## Prohibited Uses

### ‚ùå Treasure Hunting and Looting
**STRICTLY PROHIBITED**:
- Using ArcheoScope results to locate artifacts for collection or sale
- Providing coordinates to metal detectorists or artifact hunters
- Commercial exploitation of archaeological sites
- Any activity that damages or destroys archaeological contexts

**Legal Note**: Artifact hunting and site looting are illegal in most jurisdictions and violate international heritage protection conventions.

### ‚ùå Unauthorized Site Access
**STRICTLY PROHIBITED**:
- Accessing private property without permission
- Entering protected archaeological areas without authorization
- Violating cultural or religious site restrictions
- Ignoring local access protocols and regulations

### ‚ùå Coordinate Publication
**STRICTLY PROHIBITED**:
- Publishing precise GPS coordinates of sensitive sites
- Sharing detailed location data on public platforms
- Creating maps that enable unauthorized site access
- Distributing location information to unverified parties

### ‚ùå Cultural Appropriation
**STRICTLY PROHIBITED**:
- Claiming "discovery" of sites known to local communities
- Ignoring indigenous or traditional knowledge about sites
- Conducting research without appropriate cultural consultation
- Misrepresenting or commercializing cultural heritage
