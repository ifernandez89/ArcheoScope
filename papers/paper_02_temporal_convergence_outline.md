## ðŸ§  Conceptual Framework

### The Convergence Principle
Traditional archaeological remote sensing relies on single-source evidence (LIDAR-only, satellite-only). This paper establishes **temporal convergence** as a validation framework where:

1. **Multiple independent sensors** observe the same archaeological features
2. **Temporal consistency** across different time periods strengthens evidence
3. **Spatial convergence** of different evidence types increases confidence
4. **Quantitative metrics** enable reproducible validation

### Beyond Single-Site Analysis
While Paper 1 demonstrated functional persistence at Angkor, Paper 2 expands to:
- **Multiple archaeological contexts** (Maya, Roman, Mesopotamian)
- **Different environmental conditions** (tropical, temperate, arid)
- **Varying temporal depths** (500-3000 years)
- **Cross-cultural validation** of methodology

## ðŸ“Š Proposed Case Studies

### Case Study 1: Maya Lowlands (Guatemala/Belize)
**Site**: Caracol and surrounding landscape  
**LIDAR Data**: Available (Chase et al., 2011)  
**Archaeological Context**: Well-documented urban center with hydraulic systems  
**Temporal Depth**: ~1200 years since abandonment  
**Environmental Challenge**: Dense tropical forest, similar to Angkor  

**Expected Results**:
- Canal systems: High temporal persistence expected
- Terraces: Moderate persistence (erosion effects)
- Urban areas: Low persistence (forest reclamation)

### Case Study 2: Roman Aqueducts (Mediterranean)
**Site**: Pont du Gard region, Southern France  
**LIDAR Data**: Available through French national program  
**Archaeological Context**: Extensive Roman hydraulic infrastructure  
**Temporal Depth**: ~1800 years since construction  
**Environmental Challenge**: Mediterranean climate, modern development  

**Expected Results**:
- Aqueduct channels: High persistence (stone construction)
- Distribution networks: Moderate persistence (urban overlay)
- Agricultural terraces: Variable persistence (continued use)

### Case Study 3: Mesopotamian Irrigation (Iraq)
**Site**: Ancient Babylon region  
**LIDAR Data**: Limited, rely on satellite analysis  
**Archaeological Context**: Earliest urban hydraulic systems  
**Temporal Depth**: ~3000+ years  
**Environmental Challenge**: Arid environment, modern irrigation overlay  

**Expected Results**:
- Major canals: High persistence (continued water flow)
- Field systems: Low persistence (modern agriculture)
- Urban areas: Minimal persistence (continuous occupation)

#### Temporal Scaling
- **Recent abandonment** (0-500 years): High persistence expected
- **Medieval abandonment** (500-1500 years): Moderate persistence
- **Ancient abandonment** (1500+ years): Low but detectable persistence

### Advanced Convergence Analysis

#### Bayesian Evidence Integration
- **Prior probabilities**: Based on archaeological knowledge
- **Likelihood functions**: Derived from sensor characteristics
- **Posterior probabilities**: Integrated evidence assessment
- **Uncertainty quantification**: Confidence intervals for all results

#### Machine Learning Validation
- **Training data**: Known archaeological features across all sites
- **Feature extraction**: Automated pattern recognition
- **Cross-validation**: Leave-one-site-out testing
- **Performance metrics**: Precision, recall, F1-score by site type
