## üìä Competitive Analysis

### Existing Literature Gaps
1. **Static vs. Dynamic**: Current methods focus on structure detection, not functional persistence
2. **Temporal Integration**: Limited integration of multi-temporal satellite data with LIDAR
3. **Quantitative Metrics**: Lack of standardized metrics for archaeological system vitality
4. **Living Archaeology**: Concept of functional persistence largely unexplored

### Competitive Advantages
1. **Novel Question**: Shift from "what's there?" to "what's still functional?"
2. **Quantitative Framework**: Reproducible metrics and thresholds
3. **Multi-Layer Convergence**: Independent evidence sources increase confidence
4. **Benchmark Case**: Angkor provides ideal validation opportunity
5. **Practical Applications**: Clear implications for water management and restoration

### Potential Criticisms and Responses

**Criticism**: "Limited validation - only one site"
**Response**: Angkor provides ideal benchmark due to extensive existing data; methodology is designed for broader application

**Criticism**: "Functional persistence is subjective"
**Response**: Quantitative metrics (FPI) provide objective assessment; thresholds are empirically derived

**Criticism**: "Satellite resolution limitations"
**Response**: 10m resolution adequate for landscape-scale infrastructure; method complements rather than replaces high-resolution LIDAR

**Criticism**: "Temporal window too short (7 years)"
**Response**: Demonstrates proof-of-concept; longer time series will strengthen future applications
